### IT Podcast by Code Decode: From Tier 3 College to Technical Architect

**Episode Overview**
This podcast features an in-depth conversation with a seasoned IT professional who transitioned from a Tier 3 engineering college to the prestigious role of a Technical Architect. The discussion highlights the strategies, technical roadmap, and mindset shifts required to achieve a 700% salary hike over several years of a career.

---

### 1. The Starting Point: Tier 3 Challenges
*   **Initial Struggles:** Graduates from Tier 3 colleges often face a lack of campus placements in top-tier product companies.
*   **Starting Salary:** The guest discusses starting at a very modest package (typical of service-based companies) and the realization that skills, not college tags, determine long-term success.
*   **The Mindset:** The transition requires moving from a "job-seeker" mindset to a "problem-solver" mindset.

### 2. The Career Journey & Incremental Growth
*   **The First Switch:** The most difficult jump is the first one—moving from a service-based environment to a product-based or high-growth startup.
*   **Strategic Job Hopping:** The 700% hike was not achieved in a single jump but through 3-4 strategic switches, each providing a 50% to 100% increase by leveraging competing offers.
*   **Internal Growth:** While switching provides the hike, staying long enough to own a module provides the depth required for the Architect role.

### 3. Technical Roadmap to Becoming an Architect
To reach a Technical Architect level, the guest emphasizes moving beyond "just coding" to "designing systems."

*   **Java/Spring Boot Mastery:** Deep diving into Java internals (JVM, Garbage Collection, Multi-threading) and Spring Boot's underlying architecture.
*   **Microservices:** Understanding the "why" behind microservices—Saga patterns, Circuit Breakers, and Event-driven architecture (Kafka/RabbitMQ).
*   **System Design (HLD & LLD):**
    *   **High-Level Design (HLD):** Focusing on scalability, availability, and reliability. Understanding Load Balancers, Caching (Redis), and Database Sharding.
    *   **Low-Level Design (LLD):** Mastering Design Patterns (Singleton, Factory, Strategy) and SOLID principles to write clean, maintainable code.
*   **Cloud & DevOps:** Familiarity with AWS/Azure and basic CI/CD pipelines is no longer optional for senior roles.

### 4. Keys to the 700% Salary Hike
*   **In-Depth Knowledge:** During interviews, don't just explain "how" to use a tool; explain "how it works internally." This differentiates a Senior Dev from an Architect.
*   **Negotiation Skills:** Always have multiple offers in hand. The guest explains how to use an existing offer to negotiate a higher "market correction" with the next company.
*   **Niche Skills:** Specializing in high-demand areas like high-concurrency systems or complex migrations makes a candidate indispensable.

### 5. Responsibilities of a Technical Architect
The role changes significantly from a Senior Developer:
*   **Decision Making:** Choosing the right database (SQL vs. NoSQL) based on the specific use case.
*   **Cost Optimization:** Designing systems that are not only fast but cost-efficient in terms of cloud resources.
*   **Mentorship:** Reviewing code and guiding junior/mid-senior developers on best practices.
*   **Stakeholder Communication:** Bridging the gap between business requirements and technical implementation.

### 6. Critical Interview Advice
*   **The "Why" Factor:** In Architect interviews, you are rarely asked to write a sorting algorithm. Instead, you are asked why you chose a specific technology over another.
*   **Project Depth:** Be prepared to draw the entire architecture of your current project on a whiteboard, explaining every component and bottleneck.
*   **Handling Failure:** The guest notes that every "rejected" interview was a lesson that identified a gap in their knowledge, which they filled before the next attempt.

### 7. Final Tips for Aspirants
*   **Consistency:** Spend at least one hour every day upskilling, even if you are not looking for a job.
*   **Community/Networking:** Engage with platforms like LinkedIn to understand market trends and what recruiters are looking for.
*   **Don't Settle:** A Tier 3 background is only a hurdle for the first 2 years. After that, your "Project Portfolio" is your resume.
